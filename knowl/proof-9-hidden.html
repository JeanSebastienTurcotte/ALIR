Soit \(\vec{u},\vec{v}\in \R^n\) et \(r\in\R\text{.}\) Posons \(\vec{c}_1,\vec{c}_2,\ldots , \vec{c}_n\) les colonnes de \(A\text{.}\) Alors on a
\begin{align*} T(\vec{u}+\vec{v})&=A(\vec{u}+\vec{v})\\ &=\begin{pmatrix} \lvert & \lvert & \cdots & \lvert \\ \vec{c}_1 & \vec{c}_2 & \cdots & \vec{c}_n \\ \lvert & \lvert & \cdots & \lvert \end{pmatrix}\left(\begin{pmatrix}u_1\\ u_2\\ \vdots\\ u_n \end{pmatrix} +\begin{pmatrix}v_1\\ v_2\\ \vdots\\ v_n \end{pmatrix}\right)\\ &=\begin{pmatrix} \lvert & \lvert & \cdots & \lvert \\ \vec{c}_1 & \vec{c}_2 & \cdots & \vec{c}_n \\ \lvert & \lvert & \cdots & \lvert \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}u_1+v_1\\ u_2+v_2\\ \vdots\\ u_n+v_n \end{pmatrix}\\ &=(u_1+v_1)\vec{c}_1+(u_2+v_2)\vec{c}_2+\cdots +(u_n+v_n)\vec{c}_n &\text{selon l'équation }& \knowl{./knowl/eq-matvecprodgen.html}{\text{(2.1.7)}}\\ &=u_1\vec{c}_1+v_1\vec{c}_1+u_2\vec{c}_2+v_2\vec{c}_2+\cdots +u_n\vec{c}_n+v_n\vec{c}_n\\ &=u_1\vec{c}_1+u_2\vec{c}_2+\cdots+u_n\vec{c}_n+v_1\vec{c}_1+v_2\vec{c}_2+\cdots +v_n\vec{c}_n\\ &=A\vec{u}+A\vec{v} &\text{selon l'équation } & \knowl{./knowl/eq-matvecprodgen.html}{\text{(2.1.7)}}\\ &=T(\vec{u})+T(\vec{v}) \end{align*}
et
\begin{align*} T(r\vec{u})&=A(r\vec{u})\\ &=\begin{pmatrix} \lvert & \lvert & \cdots & \lvert \\ \vec{c}_1 & \vec{c}_2 & \cdots & \vec{c}_n \\ \lvert & \lvert & \cdots & \lvert \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}ru_1\\ ru_2\\ \vdots\\ ru_n \end{pmatrix}\\ &=ru_1\vec{c}_1+ru_2\vec{c}_2+\cdots +ru_n\vec{c}_n && \text{selon l'équation } \knowl{./knowl/eq-matvecprodgen.html}{\text{(2.1.7)}}\\ &=r(u_1\vec{c}_1+u_2\vec{c}_2+\cdots +u_n\vec{c}_n)\\ &=rA\vec{u} && \text{selon l'équation } \knowl{./knowl/eq-matvecprodgen.html}{\text{(2.1.7)}}\\ &=rT(\vec{u})\text{.} \end{align*}
Toute matrice correspond donc à une transformation linéaire.
