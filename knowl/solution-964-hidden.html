Il faut trouver \(a,b\) tels que
\begin{equation*} \vecd{0}{1}=a\vecd{\lambda_1}{1}+b\vecd{\lambda_2}{1}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Sous forme matricielle, on cherche à résoudre la matrice augmentée
\begin{equation*} \left(\begin{array}{cc|c} \lambda_1 & \lambda_2 & 0\\ 1&1&1\end{array}\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
On a
\begin{align*} \left(\begin{array}{cc|c} \lambda_1 & \lambda_2 & 0\\ 1&1&1\end{array}\right)&\begin{array}{l}L_2-L_1/\lambda_1\to L_2\\ \sim \end{array} \left(\begin{array}{cc|c}\lambda_1 & \lambda_2 & 0\\ 0& 1-\lambda_2/\lambda_1 &1 \end{array}\right)\\ &=\left(\begin{array}{cc|c}\lambda_1 & \lambda_2 & 0\\ 0& 1+\lambda_2^2 &1 \end{array}\right) && \text{ selon } \frac{1}{\lambda_1}=-\lambda_2\\ &\begin{array}{l}L2/(1+\lambda_2^2)\to L_2 \\ \sim\end{array}\left(\begin{array}{cc|c}\lambda_1 & \lambda_2 & 0\\ 0& 1 &\frac{1}{1+\lambda_2^2} \end{array}\right)\\ &\begin{array}{l}L1-\lambda_2L_2\to L_1 \\ \sim\end{array}\left(\begin{array}{cc|c}\lambda_1 & \lambda_2 & -\frac{\lambda_2}{1+\lambda_2^2}\\ 0& 1 &\frac{1}{1+\lambda_2^2} \end{array}\right)\\ &\begin{array}{l}L_1/\lambda_1\to L_1 \\ \sim \end{array}\left(\begin{array}{cc|c}1 & \lambda_2/\lambda_1 & -\frac{\lambda_2}{\lambda_1(1+\lambda_2^2)}\\ 0& 1 &\frac{1}{1+\lambda_2^2} \end{array}\right)\\ &=\left(\begin{array}{cc|c}1 & -\lambda_2^2 & \frac{\lambda_2^2}{1+\lambda_2^2}\\ 0& 1 &\frac{1}{1+\lambda_2^2} \end{array}\right)&& \text{ car } \frac{1}{\lambda_1}=-\lambda_2\text{.} \end{align*}
Un peu de simplification est de mise. On peut montrer que \(\frac{1}{1+\lambda_2^2}=-\frac{\sqrt{5}(1-\sqrt{5})}{10}=-\frac{\sqrt{5}\lambda_1}{5}\) et que \(\frac{\lambda_2^2}{1+\lambda_2^2}=\frac{\sqrt{5}(1+\sqrt{5})}{10}=\frac{\sqrt{5}\lambda_2}{5}\text{.}\) On a donc
\begin{equation*} \vecd{0}{1}=\frac{\sqrt{5}\lambda_2}{5}\vec{v}_1-\frac{\sqrt{5}\lambda_1}{5}\vec{v}_2\text{.} \end{equation*}
